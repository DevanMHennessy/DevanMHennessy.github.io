Sarracenia Trumpet Plant

What is it?

Sarracenia, commonly known as North American pitcher plants or trumpet pitchers, are native to Canada and the Eastern United States.

These stunning plants form tall, tubular leaves that act as pitfall traps. Insects are lured by sweet smelling nectar and bright colors, then crawl down into the pitchers. Tiny downward pointing hairs trap and pierce the prey for digestion.

General Care Guide

Light:

At least 6 hours (or more) of full sun. More light encourages darker pigmentation.

Water:

Use distilled water. Keep in a tray of water 1-2 inches deep year-round.

Humidity:

Moderate to high (50-70%). Outdoor growing is ideal.

Temperature:

Tolerates -6-32°C. Needs winter dormancy below 10°C but avoid long term freezing.

Soil:

50/50 peat moss and perlite with added sand.

Feeding

Natural Diet:

Catches wasps, flies, ants, and other insects naturally. Insatiable appetite!

Manual Feeding:

Not needed if outdoors. Indoors, can add small insects or fish pellets every 2-4 weeks.

What to Avoid:

No fertilizer, meat, or human food. Only insects.

Winter Care:

Stop feeding during dormancy. Feeding during cold months causes mold growth and can harm the plant.

Tips, Tricks, and Recommendations

  • • Sarracenia LOVE full sun - give them lots of light
  • • Never use tap water - only distilled, rainwater, or reverse osmosis water
  • • Avoid strong winds. Use tomato cones on taller trumpet species to help keep upright
  • • Winter dormancy is CRITICAL - they need 3-4 months of cold temps below 10°C
  • • West coast growers can keep them outside year-round in most areas
  • • Old pitchers turning brown and dieing is normal - trim them off at the base in spring if desired
  • • Best beginner species: S. purpurea, S. leucophylla, S. flava, S. oreophila
  • • Flowers are beautiful! Let them bloom, but cut stem after flowering to encourage healthy growth structure
