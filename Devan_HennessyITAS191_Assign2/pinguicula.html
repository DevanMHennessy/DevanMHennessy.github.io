What is it?

Pinguicula, commonly known as butterworts, are carnivorous plants with over 80 species found across the Northern Hemisphere, with many in Mexico and Central America.

Their leaves are covered in sticky mucilage that traps small insects. The leaves slowly curl around prey to maximize digestion. They produce stunning flowers in vibrant colors - some say they're the most beautiful carnivorous plants!

General Care Guide

Light:

Bright indirect light. More direct light encourages red pigmentation while less light encourages green.

Water:

Distilled water. Let soil dry slightly between waterings.

Humidity:

Moderate to high (50-70%). Mexican species tolerate lower humidity.

Temperature:

Tropical: 15-25°C. Temperate: need cool winter rest for 3 months (4-10°C).

Soil:

50/50 peat and added perlite or orchid bark, or pure sharp sand for Mexican species.

Feeding

Natural Diet:

Catches gnats, fruit flies, aphids, and small flying insects (like mosquitoes).

Manual Feeding:

Can place tiny insects on leaves or small amounts of fine ground (or prehydrated) fish pellets.

What to Avoid:

No large insects, meat, or touching the sticky leaves unnecessarily.

Seasonal Changes:

Mexican species form non-carnivorous succulent leaves in winter.

Tips, Tricks, and Recommendations

  • • Mexican pings (P. moranensis, P. agnata) are PERFECT for beginners
  • • Unlike other carnivorous plants, pings prefer soil to dry slightly between waterings
  • • The flowers are incredible - let them bloom! They don't weaken the plant and are beautiful
  • • Sticky leaves mean the plant is healthy - if leaves lose stickiness, increase light and humidity
  • • West coast growers: Mexican species thrive in mild climates, but not frost hardy
  • • Many Mexican pings naturally form succulent leaves in winter - this is normal!
  • • Great for windowsill growing - don't need as much light as Venus flytraps
  • • Propagate easily from leaf pullings - gently remove a leaf and place ontop of soil
