What is it?

Dionaea muscipula, the Venus flytrap, is perhaps the most iconic carnivorous plant in the world. Native only to a small region in North and South Carolina, USA.

Their modified leaves form snap traps with trigger hairs inside. When an insect touches two hairs within 20 seconds, the trap snaps shut in less than a second! The plant then digests its prey over 5-12 days before reopening.

General Care Guide

Light:

Full sun, 4-6 hours minimum. The more sun the better!

Water:

Distilled water only. Likes to stay wet but not water logged.

Humidity:

Moderate around 50%. Outdoor growing is best.

Temperature:

10-30°C. MUST have winter dormancy (below 10°C for 3-4 months).

Soil:

50/50 peat moss and perlite or sand added for drainage. No fertilizer needed.

Feeding

Natural Diet:

Catches spiders, ants, flies, and small crawling insects.

Manual Feeding:

Can feed live insects. One trap per month is enough for entire plant.

What to Avoid:

Do not feed meat, dead bugs, or trigger traps for fun.

Trap Lifespan:

Each trap closes about 5 times before dying. New traps are grown often.

Tips, Tricks, and Recommendations

  • • DON'T trigger the traps for fun - each trap has limited energy!
  • • Winter dormancy is CRITICAL - they will die without 3-4 months of cold
  • • Black traps are normal and healthy - avoid removal if possible
  • • Never use tap water - minerals will kill the plant within months
  • • West coast: keep outdoors year-round, avoid freezing temperatures
  • • If a trap doesn't close, it's either too old or wasn't triggered properly
  • • Remove flower stalks unless you want seeds - flowering weakens the plant
  • • Try bottom watering for best watering practice
