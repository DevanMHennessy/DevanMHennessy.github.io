What is it?

Nepenthes, commonly known as tropical pitcher plants, are carnivorous plants native to Southeast Asia, Madagascar, and Australia.

They capture prey using modified leaves that form deep pitchers filled with digestive fluid and a slick opening that bugs can't crawl out of. These plants grow in nutrient-poor soils and supplement their diet by trapping insects.

General Care Guide

Light:

Bright, indirect light. Avoid direct afternoon sun.

Water:

Use distilled water. Keep soil moist but not water logged.

Humidity:

High humidity (60-80%). Mist regularly.

Temperature:

20-30°C in the day, 15-20°C at night.

Soil:

1 part Sphagnum moss, 1 part perlite, and 1 part orchid bark mix.

Feeding

Natural Diet:

Catches flying and crawling insects naturally.

Manual Feeding:

Not necessary, but can feed small insects or fish pellets every 2-4 weeks.

What to Avoid:

Never fertilize, feed pitchers meat or large insects as it increases risk of rot.

Pitcher Fluid:

If dry, add distilled water to fill 1/3 full but do so sparingly.

Tips, Tricks, and Recommendations

  • • Never use tap water - minerals will harm the plant
  • • May require yearly pruning due to tendency to grow vertically
  • • Drapes off trees in nature, makes great candidate for hanging plants
  • • When old pitchers reach over 50% shrivelled, just trim them off
  • • Start with easier species like N. ventricosa or N. sanguinea
  • • Consider a greenhouse or terrarium for consistent humid conditions
  • • On the west coast, outdoor growing is possible as long as freezing temps are avoided
  • • Misting in the morning helps prevent mold growth
