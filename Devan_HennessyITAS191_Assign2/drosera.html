What is it?

Drosera, commonly known as sundews, are one of the largest genera of carnivorous plants with over 194 species found on every continent except Antarctica.

Their leaves are covered in sticky, glistening tentacles that sparkle like morning dew in the sun. Insects become trapped in the sticky mucilage, and the plant slowly curls around its prey to digest it. Beautiful and deadly!

General Care Guide

Light:

Bright light, 4-6 hours direct sun. More sun is better.

Water:

Distilled water only. Keep plant in a dish with 1-2 inches of water.

Humidity:

Moderate to high (50-80%). Do not mist or top water to avoid washing away digestive dew

Temperature:

Varies by species. Tropical (most common): 15-30°C. Temperate: need winter dormancy.

Soil:

50/50 peat moss and perlite. Can add sphagnum moss for better hydration.

Feeding

Natural Diet:

Catches gnats, fruit flies, mosquitoes, and small flying insects.

Manual Feeding:

Can place tiny insects or fish food on leaves. Plant will curl around prey in hours.

What to Avoid:

No large insects, meat, or touching the sticky dew unnecessarily.

Dew Production:

More light produces more dew. If dew disappears, increase light and humidity.

Tips, Tricks, and Recommendations

  • • Cape sundew (D. capensis) is the BEST beginner sundew - nearly indestructible
  • • If the dew disappears, your plant needs more light or humidity
  • • Never touch the sticky tentacles - they take days to regenerate dew
  • • Trim away well dead tentacles to avoid mold accumulation
  • • Tropical species (D. capensis, D. aliciae) don't need dormancy
  • • Temperate species (D. filiformis) need cold winter dormancy like Sarracenia
  • • If kept indoors year round, feed plant small fish food every 2-4 weeks
  • • Sundews produce beautiful flowers on tall stalks - let them bloom!
